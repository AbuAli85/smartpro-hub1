# SmartPRO — Pricing Negotiation Playbook

> Audience: Account managers, founders, anyone closing deals  
> Purpose: Protect margin, close with confidence, and never give a discount that doesn't buy you something in return

---

## 1. The Core Rule

> **Never discount without getting something back.**

Every concession you give must be paired with a condition:
- Discount → annual commitment
- Discount → faster close (sign this week)
- Discount → referral or testimonial
- Discount → case study permission

A discount with no condition teaches the client that the price is negotiable every time. You will be renegotiating at every renewal.

---

## 2. Price Anchor — Hold the Line First

Before any discount discussion, always restate the value:

> "The Professional plan is OMR 150/month. That's less than what a part-time accountant charges for payroll alone — and we do payroll, HR, contracts, and documents. The ROI is positive from month one."

**Value anchors by tier:**

| Tier | Your price | What it replaces | ROI framing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | OMR 60/mo | Manual Excel + accountant ad-hoc fees | "Saves 8–10 hours of HR admin per month" |
| Professional | OMR 150/mo | Accountant (OMR 150–200) + HR errors | "Replaces your payroll accountant and reduces errors" |
| Business | OMR 365/mo | HR officer (OMR 400+) + compliance consultant (OMR 200+) | "Cheaper than one mid-level HR hire — and never calls in sick" |
| Enterprise | OMR 600+/mo | Multiple tools + integration costs | "One platform, one login, one bill — instead of 4 systems" |

State this before any price objection arises. It makes the objection harder to raise.

---

## 3. Discount Authority Matrix

Only offer discounts within these bounds. Going below floor requires founder/director approval.

| Tier | List price (monthly) | Max discount (%) | Floor price (monthly) | Condition required |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | OMR 45–75 | 15% | OMR 40 | Annual billing |
| Professional | OMR 120–180 | 15% | OMR 102 | Annual billing |
| Business | OMR 280–450 | 12% | OMR 246 | Annual + early sign |
| Enterprise | Custom | Negotiated | >OMR 500 | Director approval |

**Never discount monthly billing.** Monthly is priced at a premium precisely because it has no commitment. The discount mechanism is the annual plan.

---

## 4. The Annual Billing Pitch (Your Primary Tool)

Annual billing gives you margin room and the client a real saving. Lead with this every time.

> "Our monthly plan is flexible — no commitment. But if you're confident this will work for your business, the annual plan saves you 15%. On Professional, that's OMR 270 back in your pocket over the year."

**Annual plan numbers (pre-calculated for easy reference):**

| Tier | Monthly | Annual (÷12) | Annual saving |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter (mid) | OMR 60 | OMR 51 | OMR 108/year |
| Professional (mid) | OMR 150 | OMR 127.50 | OMR 270/year |
| Business (mid) | OMR 365 | OMR 310.25 | OMR 657/year |
| Enterprise (min) | OMR 600 | OMR 510 | OMR 1,080/year |

For Business and Enterprise clients, the annual saving is a real budget argument — OMR 657/year is a meaningful number to an Omani finance manager.

---

## 5. Pilot Pricing (New Clients Only)

For clients who want to "test before they commit fully," offer a structured pilot — not an open-ended discount.

**Pilot terms:**
- Duration: 30 days (extended from the default 14-day trial)
- Price: 50% of monthly rate
- Scope: Limited to 1 department (e.g., HR only, or Payroll only)
- Condition: Signed LOI (Letter of Intent) before pilot starts

**Why an LOI?** It filters time-wasters. A client who won't sign an LOI for a discounted pilot will not convert.

**Pilot pricing by tier:**

| Tier | Full monthly | Pilot price | Pilot modules |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | OMR 60 | OMR 30 | HR + Documents |
| Professional | OMR 150 | OMR 75 | HR + Payroll (1 month run) |
| Business | OMR 365 | OMR 185 | HR + Payroll + Compliance |
| Enterprise | Custom | 50% custom rate | Scoped modules only |

At the end of the pilot, the default next step is full pricing. There is no "extend the pilot" option. Either convert or close.

## 6. The Three Price Objections (Verbatim Responses)

### Objection A: "It's too expensive"

**Step 1 — Don't panic. Ask:**
> "Compared to what?"

*They will say: Excel, their current system, hiring someone, or a competitor.*

**Step 2 — Address the specific comparison:**

If Excel:
> "Excel doesn't file WPS, calculate PASI, track Sanad renewals, or give you an audit trail. It also doesn't tell you when a labour card expires. The risk of Excel for payroll in Oman is not the cost of the software — it's the MoL fine when something goes wrong."

If competitor (e.g., Bayzat, Mena):
> "Our Professional plan is typically 15–20% less than Bayzat for the same headcount, and we're built specifically for Oman compliance — Sanad, MoL, Omanization. Can I show you a side-by-side?"

If hiring someone:
> "A mid-level HR officer in Muscat costs OMR 400–500/month in salary alone — before visa, insurance, and leave costs. Business plan is OMR 365. And SmartPRO doesn't take annual leave."

**Step 3 — Offer the trial as the close:**
> "The easiest way to answer the question is to use it for 14 days with your real data. If it doesn't save time and reduce errors, you don't pay. That's the only real test."

---

### Objection B: "Can you give us a better price?"

**Never say "I'll see what I can do."** That signals weakness.

**Say instead:**
> "Our pricing is built on what it costs to run the system reliably and support you properly. I can offer you the annual plan at 15% off — that's the best rate available for [Professional]. Would that work?"

If they push further:
> "If it's the monthly vs annual question, let me show you the annual numbers — because on Business, the saving is over OMR 650 a year. That's a real budget line."

If they are a genuinely small company and Starter is the right fit but they're comparing to Professional:
> "It sounds like Starter might actually be the right fit for you right now — OMR 45–60/month, full HR, documents, and contracts. You can upgrade to Professional when you're ready to add payroll processing."

---

### Objection C: "We need to think about it / discuss internally"

This usually means one of three things:
1. They haven't seen enough value yet
2. They need approval from someone you haven't met
3. They are genuinely interested but not the final decision-maker

**Respond:**
> "Of course. Can I ask — what's the main question on the table? Is it the pricing, or is there something about the system you're not sure about yet?"

*Let them answer. Then address the specific concern.*

> "Would it help to have a 20-minute session with your [HR director / CFO / GM] so they can see the system directly? I can be flexible on timing."

If they say "we'll get back to you" without a specific date:
> "I understand. Let's set a specific time to follow up — does [specific day and time] work for a 10-minute call to see where things stand?"

Never leave a meeting without a specific next date and time in the calendar.

## 7. Minimum Acceptable Margins

These are the floors below which a deal should not close without director approval. The onboarding fee partially offsets first-year discount costs.

| Tier | Floor monthly | Floor annual | Minimum onboarding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | OMR 40 | OMR 432 | OMR 100 |
| Professional | OMR 100 | OMR 1,080 | OMR 250 |
| Business | OMR 250 | OMR 2,700 | OMR 500 |
| Enterprise | OMR 500 | OMR 6,000 | OMR 1,500 |

Below these floors: escalate to founder/director. Do not close independently.

---

## 8. When to Walk Away

A deal is not worth closing if:
- The client wants Enterprise modules at Starter pricing with no path to upgrade
- They want to skip the onboarding fee and then complain the system is too hard
- They want a pilot with no LOI and no timeline for decision
- They've been "thinking about it" for more than 30 days with no specific objection

In these cases, a firm but warm close-out:
> "I understand this may not be the right moment for your company. Our offer stands — when the timing is right, reach out and we can set up a fresh trial. We'd genuinely love to work with you."

This leaves the door open without burning your time.

---

## 9. Renewal Negotiation

At renewal, the default is to hold price. Price increases are justified when:
- A new module was added to their package
- Headcount grew materially (> 20% more employees)
- A major platform upgrade was released during the year

**Handling renewal objection ("the price went up"):**
> "Your headcount grew from 30 to 45 employees during the year — we're actually still at the same per-employee rate. If anything, you're getting more value per rial as you grow."

Annual plan renewals: offer a 30-day early-renewal window with a price-lock guarantee:
> "If you renew before [date], we lock in this year's rate for another 12 months — regardless of any future price changes."

## 11. Cultural Notes — Oman Negotiation Context

- **Relationship first.** Oman business culture values trust and personal rapport highly. Do not rush to pricing. Spend time on discovery and the relationship.
- **Never appear desperate.** Offering discounts unsolicited signals the product isn't worth the price. Wait for the client to raise price before discussing it.
- **Majlis culture.** Decisions often involve consultation with partners or family members in SMEs. The person in the meeting may not be the final decision-maker. Always ask.
- **Wasta (connections).** A referral from a trusted mutual contact is worth 10 cold calls. If you know someone in their network, mention it early.
- **Patience with timelines.** Omani businesses may take longer to decide than Western counterparts. Follow up consistently but without pressure. Weekly check-ins are appropriate.
- **Avoid high-pressure closes.** "This offer expires today" tactics damage relationships in the Omani context. Use scarcity only when it's real (e.g., limited onboarding slots).
- **Arabic in the contract.** For formal agreements, offer an Arabic version of the service agreement. It signals respect and professionalism.
