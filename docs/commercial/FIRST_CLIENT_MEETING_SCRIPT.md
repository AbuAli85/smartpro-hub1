# SmartPRO — First Client Meeting Script

> Format: 30-minute in-person or video meeting  
> Goal: Qualify, build trust, show one live demo moment, agree on a next step  
> Never: pitch before you listen. Never: show everything. One wow moment wins more than ten features.

---

## Before the Meeting (5 minutes of prep)

Research the company:
- How many employees? (Sets the package conversation)
- What industry? (Construction/services/trading = compliance pain. Professional services = payroll + contracts pain)
- Do they have a CR number on their website? (Means they're formalised — speak compliance)
- Did they come from a referral or cold? (Adjust warmth of opening)

Set up on your laptop:
- Demo tenant logged in as **Fatma** (HR Admin — Business tier, Al Noor Gulf LLC)
- Platform Admin tab open but minimised
- Have the package switching ready (Settings → Package → change to Starter)

---

## Minute 0–3 — Opening (Build Rapport)

**In Arabic if the meeting is Arabic-first:**

> "أهلاً وسهلاً، شاكرين لكم وقتكم. أنا [اسمك] من سمارت برو. هل تناولتم القهوة؟"
> *(Welcome, thank you for your time. I'm [your name] from SmartPRO. Have you had coffee?)*

**Then in English or Arabic, depending on their preference:**

> "Before I show you anything, I'd like to understand your business for five minutes — because what I show you should be relevant to you, not just a generic demo."

This line does two things: it signals you're not a typical salesperson, and it buys you the discovery time you need.

---

## Minute 3–13 — Discovery (Listen More Than You Talk)

Ask these questions in this order. Do not skip. Each answer tells you which module to lead with.

### Q1 — Size and structure
> "How many employees do you have right now, and do you have both Omani nationals and expats?"

*Listen for:* headcount (sets the package tier), expat ratio (signals Sanad/compliance pain), growth plans.

### Q2 — Current tools
> "Right now, how do you handle your monthly payroll — Excel, a system, or through an accountant?"

*Listen for:* Excel + WhatsApp = immediate Professional/Business pitch. Existing system = ask "what's missing from it?"

### Q3 — Biggest operational headache
> "If I asked your HR manager or finance manager what takes the most time every month, what would they say?"

*Listen for:* payroll errors, leave tracking, contract chasing, compliance renewals, Omanization pressure.

### Q4 — Compliance situation
> "Are you tracking your WPS submissions and Omanization ratio manually, or do you have a system for that?"

*Listen for:* "manually" = business or enterprise tier conversation. "We have a problem with it" = instant hook.

### Q5 — Decision dynamics (critical)
> "Who else would be involved in a decision like this — is it yourself, or would HR and finance also weigh in?"

*Listen for:* sole decision-maker (move faster), committee (leave-behind doc matters more), IT involvement (mention hosted GCC, security, audit log).

---

**After discovery, pause and summarize back:**

> "So if I understand correctly — you have [X] employees, payroll is done in Excel, and the biggest pain is [their answer]. Is that right?"

This builds instant credibility. They feel heard.

---

## Minute 13–24 — The Demo (One Story, One Wow)

Do not demo everything. Pick one thread based on what you heard.

### Thread A — If pain is payroll / WPS

Start as **Khalid** (Finance Admin):
1. Show the payroll run screen — "One click processes all [X] employees"
2. Point to PASI calculation: "Auto-calculated for each Omani national — no manual formula"
3. Show the WPS file export: "This file goes straight to Bank Muscat — no re-entry"
4. Show the Arabic payslip: "Your employees get this in their portal — Arabic and English"

Then switch to Platform Admin and change package from Business → Starter:
> "Watch what happens to Khalid's screen when a company is on the basic plan — payroll disappears. When they upgrade, it unlocks instantly. Every client gets exactly what they pay for, nothing more, nothing less."

**That line closes deals.**

---

### Thread B — If pain is HR / Omanization

Start as **Fatma** (HR Admin):
1. Open the employee list — show Omani/expat breakdown
2. Show the Omanization ratio bar: "You're at 31%, target is 35% — the system flags it before MoL does"
3. Show leave approval: "One click — and the employee sees it in their portal immediately"
4. Generate a salary certificate in Arabic: "Printed and signed-quality in 30 seconds — no Word template, no printing, no scanning"

---

### Thread C — If pain is compliance / Sanad

Start as **Khalid** (Finance Admin), navigate to Compliance:
1. Show Sanad expat tracker: "12 expats, 3 renewals due — you see it before it becomes a fine"
2. Show MoL compliance status: "Warning on one labour card — system shows you who and when to act"
3. Show audit log: "Every action is logged. If MoL ever asks who approved what, you have the answer."

---

### Always finish the demo with the client portal (30 seconds):

Switch to **Sara** (Client):
> "And this is what your external clients or contractors see — only their data. Nothing from your internal HR or payroll is visible to them. Role-based, by design."

## Minute 24–28 — Handle the Three Objections

### Objection 1: "It's expensive"

> "I understand. Let me ask — what does your current situation cost you? An accountant for payroll is OMR 100–200 a month. A part-time HR person is OMR 350+. Professional plan is OMR 120–180 and replaces both — and it doesn't make calculation errors."

If they still push:
> "We have a 14-day free trial — no credit card. You test it with your real data and see if it saves time. If it doesn't, you don't pay."

---

### Objection 2: "We already have a system / we use [other tool]"

> "What's the main thing your current system doesn't do that you wish it did?"

*Let them answer. Then:*
> "That's exactly what we built for. Would it be worth 14 days to compare?"

---

### Objection 3: "We're not ready yet"

> "I hear that a lot. Can I ask — what would need to happen for you to feel ready? Is it the team, the data, the timing?"

*They will tell you the real blocker. Address it directly.*

Most common real answer: "We need to clean up our employee data first."
Response: > "Our onboarding includes a data import session. We've helped companies clean and import years of Excel data in one afternoon. That part we handle together."

---

## Minute 28–30 — Close the Next Step (Never Leave Without One)

Do not close the deal in the first meeting. Close the next step.

Options, in order of strength:

**Option 1 — Trial (strongest):**
> "Can we set you up on a 14-day trial this week? I'll personally do the onboarding call with your HR manager — takes 90 minutes."

**Option 2 — Second meeting with a decision-maker:**
> "Would it make sense to have a 20-minute session with [HR director / CFO / GM] so they can see this directly?"

**Option 3 — Leave-behind and follow up:**
> "I'll send you our one-pager and pricing sheet today. Can I call you Thursday to answer any questions?"

---

**Closing line (always say this):**

> "Our goal is simple — give you exactly what you need to run your company better, not to sell you something you don't need. That's why we start with a free trial."

In Arabic:
> "هدفنا بسيط — نعطيكم ما تحتاجونه بالضبط لتشغيل شركتكم بشكل أفضل، وليس بيعكم شيئاً لا تحتاجونه. لذلك نبدأ بتجربة مجانية."

## Industry-Specific Adjustments

### Construction / Engineering firms
- Lead with: Compliance thread (Sanad expats, labour cards, MoL)
- Key fear: MoL fine, Omanization shortfall
- Key proof: "You'll know your compliance status before the inspector does"

### Trading companies / Retail
- Lead with: Payroll thread (WPS, PASI, simple payslips)
- Key fear: WPS rejection, bank rejections
- Key proof: "WPS file generated in one click — tested with Bank Muscat format"

### Professional services (accounting, law, consulting)
- Lead with: Contracts + HR thread
- Key fear: Contract disputes, document loss
- Key proof: "Every contract is timestamped, version-controlled, and e-signed — court-ready"

### Oil & gas services / Logistics
- Lead with: Business tier — full stack
- Key fear: Cost overruns, expat management, MoL
- Key proof: Show Finance KPIs + Compliance in 5 minutes

---

## What NOT to Do in the First Meeting

- Do not show all 8 modules — you will overwhelm them
- Do not start with pricing — always qualify first
- Do not say "we have many features" — show one specific feature that solves their specific pain
- Do not book a demo and then only talk — ask questions, then show
- Do not leave without a defined next step — "I'll think about it" is a lost deal
- Do not translate everything awkwardly if they prefer Arabic — switch fully to Arabic and stay there
